How Landslide Reporter Works

Your goal as a citizen scientist is to submit landslides that you find in media or in person to the Global Landslide Catalog in order to improve it for better landslide modeling and prediction and future research. The main steps to this submission process are:

  1. Landslide occurs: A landslide event occurs and is reported in a newspaper article, online database, or in your local area.
  2. Submit to Landslide Reporter: Use Landslide Reporter describe and add an event to the database.
  3. Approval: Our team checks all of the incoming landslide reports for accuracy.
  4. View Your Landslide: Once approved, your landslide event appears on Landslide Viewer alongside other landslide databases like the GLC.
  5. Download and Use: Scientists and the public can download the landslide data from Landslide Viewer to use in research, models, and policy work!

For more information about how you can make your landslide submission today, please refer to the Navigation on the right.

Accessing the Application

Landslide Reporter can be accessed by either your computer’s or your mobile phone’s internet browser. You will need a Facebook account or Google+ account to log in. Logging in will allow you to access your previous submissions. Your credentials will not be linked to your reports in the online database.

Upon signing in, you will be presented with the Terms and Conditions of using Landslide Reporter. Closing the box indicates you have accepted the Terms and Conditions.

Once logged in, your browser may ask you to use your location. Click “Accept” or “Allow” so that the browser can pinpoint your current location, making it easier for you to submit local events.

Using Landslide Reporter on the web

Main screen

  1. Map navigation: Zoom and search for locations
  2. Username: Opens a drop-down menu to see your previous submissions or log out of your account.
  3. Terms and Conditions: Opens the Terms and Conditions for using Landslide Reporter.
  4. Landslide Reporter Map Sidebar: The sidebar for submitting new reports to the database.
  5. Red points: Your previously-submitted landslide reports.
  6. Orange points: Points added to the Cooperative Open Online Landslide Repository (COOLR).

Downloading the Catalog

The Global Landslide Catalog (GLC) can be downloaded from Landslide Viewer by clicking on the link “Download the full Landslide Catalog” at the top of the application. A new window will open with the information for downloading the GLC and the option to save it to your computer as a .gdb file.

If you use the GLC in your research publication, we request that you reference the source of the data in any publication using the DOIs below:

  • Kirschbaum, D.B., Stanley, T., & Zhou, Y. (2015). Spatial and temporal analysis of a global landslide catalog. Geomorphology, 249, 4-15. doi:10.1016/j.geomorph.2015.03.016
  • Kirschbaum, D.B., Adler, R., Hong, Y., Hill, S., & Lerner-Lam, A. (2010). A global landslide catalog for hazard applications: method, results, and limitations. Natural Hazards, 52, 561-575. doi:10.1007/s11069-009-9401-4


Any questions about citing the Global Landslide Catalog can be directed towards Dalia Kirschbaum, dalia.b.kirschbaum@nasa.gov.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I edit/delete my report after I submitted it?
    If the submission hasn’t been checked by our team yet, you may edit it under My Submissions. Click on the report you want to edit, and click the pencil icon or trash icon in the top right-hand corner to edit or delete the report.
Are the submissions quality-checked before they’re added to the catalog?
    Yes, all reports submitted through Landslide Reporter are quality-checked by members of our lab at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center for accuracy.
