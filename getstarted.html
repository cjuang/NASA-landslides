The Submission Process

Your goal as a citizen scientist is to submit landslides that you find in media or in person to the Global Landslide Catalog in order to improve it for better landslide modeling and prediction and future research. The main steps to this submission process are:

  1. Find a landslide: Browse through newspaper articles, online databases, or maybe your surroundings. Take note of where the landslide occurred, when it occurred, what type of landslide, why it occurred, what were the effects, and make note of the source you got the information from.
  2. Open the Application: Open up the Landslide Reporter Application on your computer internet browser or mobile phone internet browser. Login with Facebook or Google.
  3. Submit a Landslide Report: Once the application is loaded, click the bright green box at the bottom that reads “Submit a Landslide Report” to open the submission form. Enter as much detail as you can about the landslide event’s location, type of mass movement, and other characteristics.
  4. Validation: All you need to do is wait while our team checks all of the incoming landslide reports and approves them.
  5. View Your Landslide:Once approved, your landslide will appear on Landslide Viewer with all of the other landslides that are part of the Global Landslide Catalog (GLC). You can play around with the data and other layers on the map.
  6. Download:In Landslide Viewer, you have the option of downloading the entire Global Landslide Catalog for deeper analysis and research.

For more information about how to make a successful Landslide Reporter submission, please refer to the Navigation on the right.

Accessing the Application

The Landslide Reporter Application can be accessed by either your computer’s or your mobile phone’s internet browser. You will need a Facebook account or Google+ account to log in. Your credentials will not be made public; rather, the application wants to put all your landslide reports in one place.
