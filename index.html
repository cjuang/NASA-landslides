GET STARTED WITH COOLR




Why COOLR?

Landslides cause billions of dollars in infrastructural damage and thousands of deaths every year around the world. However, to date we do not have a global picture of exactly where and when landslides occur owing to their small size and difficulty in identifying their onset and extent when no one is around to witness their impacts. The COOLR project seeks to cultivate an open platform where scientists and citizen scientists around the world can share landslide reports to guide awareness of landslide hazards for improving scientific modeling and emergency response.

Learn more

Browse the Get Started page to learn how you can access the data, learn more about landslides, and contribute to advancing landslide science and emergency response by adding your own reports.

Hide Why are citizen scientists needed?
  • The Global Landslide Catalog (GLC) provides new insight into landslide hazards around the world, but it has been a manual and very time consuming process that is hard to maintain. In fact, if we sum up all the hours it has taken over the past 10 years to compile this database it would total over 140 days or 84 work weeks. The database is also challenged by biases affecting where and when landslide information is available (for more details please see the publications section). Therefore, we are appealing to the global citizen science community to help advance our knowledge of where and when landslides are happening around the world by adding reports to the Cooperative Open Online Landslide Repository (COOLR). Citizen scientists are people like you who can use their knowledge to advance scientific research.
  • Citizen science enables:
    1. 1. A collection with as many different sources and as little bias as possible, including first-hand accounts, reports in other languages, and points from other databases
    2. 2. More hands to contribute reports, leading to a more up-to-date and thorough database
    3. 3. More awareness and education about landslides as a natural process and natural/man-made hazard
  • With citizen science, we can improve both the quantity and quality open landslide data to provide a clearer picture of how landslides are shaping our world. Every landslide report made by you is another step towards a more predictable future!

Hide How do I add landslide events to COOLR?
  • Landslide Reporter is the submission portal for COOLR. When you find a landslide event through a news article or in-person, you can open the web application to add information about the location, date, type of landslide, and other details about the event in Landslide Reporter. See the Get Started page to start contributing to COOLR.


Hide Where can I see all landslides in COOLR?
  • All approved landslide reports, Global Landslide Catalog data, and other COOLR data appears in Landslide Viewer. You can select different layers on the map to organize and visualize the reports and view on top of with environmental information. There is also a link to download all landslide data for in-depth analysis.

Hide Can I download COOLR for my research?
  • Yes! The Cooperative Open Online Landslide Inventory (COOLR) is publicly available to download from the Landslide Viewer page. We ask that you provide a citation link to the website or, for published articles, cite the dataset using the publications referenced below.
