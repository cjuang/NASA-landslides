Overview

The Cooperative Open Landslide Inventory (COOLR) is a worldwide database of landslide events. It currently includes NASA’s Global Landslide Catalog (GLC) and citizen science data, and will soon allow for other landslide inventories. COOLR can be accessed on Landslide Viewer, and citizen scientists can contribute to COOLR through the new web application, Landslide Reporter.

LANDSLIDE VIEWER: Visualize the Data

See all approved landslide reports, Global Landslide Catalog data, and other information on the COOLR world map. Select different layers on the map to categorize the reports and view side-by-side with environmental information. Download COOLR from the viewer for more in-depth analysis.

Hide Where do we record the landslides?
  • We are recording all landslide events in the Cooperative Open Online Landslide Repository (COOLR), a central location for all open data on landslides. Currently, COOLR hosts reports made by Landslide Reporter and all reports in the Global Landslide Catalog, a database of rainfall-triggered landslides since 2007.

Hide What is the Global Landslide Catalog?
  • The GLC is the largest openly available global database of rainfall-triggered mass movements, including landslides, mudslides, debris flows, and more. The database was created in 2007 by Dr. Dalia Kirschbaum at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center, and now contains more than 11,000 reports on landslides around the world. Landslide reports come from all types of media, including news articles and other databases and have been reported by members of our lab.
  • The GLC provides open landslide hazard information to the broader community to improve basic landslide science and models, increase situational awareness, and improve disaster response. The GLC has been cited more than 75 times in peer-reviewed articles.

Hide What is citizen science?
  • According to the Oxford English Dictionary, citizen science is “scientific work undertaken by members of the general public, often in collaboration with or under the direction of professional scientists and scientific institutions.” Citizen scientists are people like you who can use their knowledge to advance scientific research.

Hide Why are citizen scientists needed to report landslides?
  • The Global Landslide Catalog (GLC) provides new insight into landslide hazards thanks to its global scope, but it is hard to maintain.
    Citizen science enables:
    1. 1. A collection with as many different sources and as little bias as possible, including first-hand accounts, reports in other languages, and points from other databases
    2. 2. More hands to contribute reports, leading to a more up-to-date and thorough database
    3. 3. Increased education about landslides as a natural process and natural/man-made hazard
  • With citizen science, we can improve both the quantity and quality of the Global Landslide Catalog to show a clearer image of how landslides are shaping our world. Every landslide report made by you is another step towards a more predictable future!

Hide Can I download the Global Landslide Catalog for my research?
  • Yes! The Global Landslide Catalog is available to download from the Landslide Viewer page.
