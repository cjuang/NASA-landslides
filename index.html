Citizen Science for Landslides

LANDSLIDE REPORTER: Submit a Landslide
Coming early 2018


You can easily add information about the location, date, type of landslide, and other details about the event using Landslide Reporter's submission tool. Once approved, the submissions are added to the Global Landslide Catalog, an open database of rainfall-triggered landslides around the world.

LANDSLIDE VIEWER: Visualize the Data


In Landslide Viewer, you can see all approved landslide reports on the world map. Selecting different layers on the map categorizes the reports over time, by type of mass movement, or by number of fatalities. You can download the GLC from the viewer or use its API for more in-depth analysis.

Frequently Asked Questions

> How do we record the landslides?

We have been recording all landslide events in the Global Landslide Catalog (GLC). The GLC is the largest openly available global database of rainfall-triggered mass movements, including landslides, mudslides, debris flows, and more. The database was created in 2007 by Dr. Dalia Kirschbaum (NASA Goddard Space Flight Center / 617), and now contains more than 11,000 reports on landslides around the world. Landslide reports come from all types of media, including news articles and other databases.

The GLC provides open landslide hazard information to the broader community to improve basic landslide science and models, increase situational awareness, and improve disaster response. The GLC has been cited more than 75 times in peer-reviewed articles.

> How can citizen scientists help?

The Global Landslide Catalog (GLC) provides new insight into landslide hazards thanks to its global scope, but it is hard to maintain.
Citizen science enables:

  1. A collection with as many different sources and as little bias as possible, including first-hand accounts, reports in other languages, and points from other databases
  2. More hands to contribute reports, leading to a more up-to-date and thorough database
  3. Increased education about landslides as a natural process and natural/man-made hazard

With citizen science, we can improve both the quantity and quality of the Global Landslide Catalog to show a clearer image of how landslides are shaping our world. Every landslide report made by you is another step towards a more predictable future!
